Work Term Report Summer 2024 - Toyota

September 11, 202410 min readMehtab Kaur

During my fourth co-op term in the summer of 2024, I transitioned to a new team as a Systems Engineering Analyst at Toyota. This experience allowed me to deepen my technical skills while exploring new tools and technologies. I will highlight the key accomplishments and challenges of my final co-op term at Toyota!

Who is TMMC

Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada (TMMC) is a world-class automotive facility with manufacturing plants located in Cambridge and Woodstock, Ontario. Their plants currently manufacture the best-selling Toyota RAV4, RAV4 Hybrid, Lexus RX 350, RX 450h, Lexus NX, and NX Hybrid. TMMC is also the most awarded manufacturing plant for quality in the Americas and has produced over 10,000,000 vehicles!

What I worked on at TMMC

During my summer 2024 term at TMMC, I had the opportunity to join a new team, which provided a fresh set of challenges and opportunities for growth. This term I took on several key projects that not only tested my technical abilities but also allowed me to make significant contributions to the team's success. Through these projects, I honed my attention to detail, enhanced my decision-making skills, and improved my ability to manage complex tasks effectively. Below, I outline the key projects I worked on and how they helped me develop both professionally and personally.

Working with Machine Learning Vision Systems

During my summer 2024 term at Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada (TMMC), I eagerly continued my work on the E-Manifest Project, a key initiative aimed at revolutionizing how vehicle build instructions are communicated on the assembly line.

To give some background, TMMC currently uses paper manifests for this purpose, which not only incurred significant indirect costs exceeding $170K annually but also posed environmental concerns.

To replace paper manifests and optimize cost efficiency, we tested low-cost technology solutions including an E-Paper display with Raspberry Pi, but encountered high image write times that exceed our acceptable threshold.

To solve this problem, I utilized the Toyota Business Practice (TBP) problem-solving tool which is used to find solutions to technical problems.

Employing a combination of Python and C programming on the Raspberry Pi, I was able to effectively deploy custom manifest image to different E-Paper models. After testing various models of the E-Paper display, I was able to identify the model with enhanced hardware and software capabilities that facilitated significantly faster image refresh times. This breakthrough led to a substantial reduction in image write times, aligning with TMMCâ€™s production line requirements while optimizing cost.

Machine Learning Life Cycle

In addition to improving the E-Paper display's performance, I developed a new application aimed at reducing downtime during hardware replacements. This tool allows team members to independently manage the replacement of E-Manifest hardware on the shop floor.

From the initial requirement gathering to final implementation, this solution aims to streamlined operations and minimized disruptions, further enhancing productivity and system reliability.

These advancements in the E-Manifest Project will not only help reduce costs and environmental impact but have also empowered team members with more autonomous and efficient tools, reinforcing TMMC's commitment to innovation and continuous improvement.

Configuring and Training Vision Systems

At the beginning of my co-op term, I was involved in testing a crucial TMMC application known as the Team Member Rotation Application (TMRA). This application plays a pivotal role in integrating team member training, attendance records, process ergo burden, and more to create and schedule daily rotations for teams and processes in each quarterly shift.

As the application improved and prepared for a new release, I actively participated in testing this application across different environments, including Development, QA, and Stage. This experience allowed me to gain a fundamental understanding of Java-based test automation tools like Selenium and TestNG. Furthermore, I had the opportunity to familiarize myself with Azure DevOps, which proved to be a valuable platform for planning, coordinating development tasks, testing code changes, and ensuring the delivery of a high-quality software product.

To monitor the application's performance during stress tests, I utilized Datadog, a monitoring and analytics tool renowned for its ability to gauge performance metrics. This software enabled me to oversee various server parameters, including CPU usage, memory allocation, disk latency, and more, under different load test scenarios.


Goals and Reflections

During my co-op term at TMMC, I focused on 3 goals that I wanted to achieve by the end of my co-op term.

Gaining experience across Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

Working on the E-Manifest allowed me to gain experience across multiple stages of the SDLC as I did a thorough analysis to understand requirements, developed a method to write custom images to the E-paper device and tested various E-Paper models to improve the image write time.

Testing emerged as another integral element of my co-op experience. My participation in testing a critical TMMC application across diverse environments and load test scenarios deepened my appreciation for the testing phase within the SDLC. I came to realize that thorough testing is paramount in ensuring the seamless functionality of an application and, ultimately, a positive end-user experience.

Beyond enhancing my understanding of various SDLC processes, this experience allowed me to actively contribute to my team's diverse tasks, fostering a sense of collaboration and accomplishment.

I eagerly anticipate the opportunity to further develop my expertise across the different phases of the SDLC in future co-op terms, knowing that this knowledge and experience is critical in the dynamic world of software development.

Strengthening my problem-solving and analytical skills

My co-op experience served as a pivotal platform for honing my problem-solving and analytical capabilities. I achieved this by leveraging the Toyota Business Practice (TBP) problem-solving methodology, which proved to be an invaluable tool for breaking down and resolving complex problems.

Applying the TBP framework allowed me to bring clarity to the E-Manifest POC project by effectively breaking down the problem, conducting a comprehensive root cause analysis, and ultimately discovering an effective solution.

Furthermore, I discovered that the applicability of the TBP methodology extended beyond the confines of a single project. I successfully employed it in diverse scenarios, enhancing my capacity to dissect problems with precision and agility.

I not only accomplished my objectives in the TBP project but also delivered a well-received presentation to a panel of judges, showcasing tangible results.
