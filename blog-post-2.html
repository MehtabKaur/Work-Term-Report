Work Term Report Summer 2023 - Toyota

September 13, 202310 min readMehtab Kaur

Hello, and welcome to my second co-op blog post! I'm Mehtab Kaur, a Software Engineering student at the University of Guelph, going into my fourth year. In this blog, I’ll be sharing an overview of my exciting four-month co-op experience during Summer 2023 as a Systems Engineering - Analyst at Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada (TMMC)!

Who is TMMC

Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada (TMMC) is a world-class automotive facility with manufacturing plants located in Cambridge and Woodstock, Ontario. Their plants currently manufacture the best-selling Toyota RAV4, RAV4 Hybrid, Lexus RX 350, RX 450h, Lexus NX, and NX Hybrid. TMMC is also the most awarded manufacturing plant for quality in the Americas and has produced over 10,000,000 vehicles!

What I worked on at TMMC

As an Analyst in the Information Systems Engineering department at TMMC, I had the opportunity to contribute to a range of projects that significantly enhanced my skill set.

Software Testing

At the beginning of my co-op term, I was involved in testing a crucial TMMC application known as the Team Member Rotation Application (TMRA). This application plays a pivotal role in integrating team member training, attendance records, process ergo burden, and more to create and schedule daily rotations for teams and processes in each quarterly shift.

As the application improved and prepared for a new release, I actively participated in testing this application across different environments, including Development, Testing, and Stage. This experience allowed me to gain a fundamental understanding of Java-based test automation tools like Selenium and TestNG. Furthermore, I had the opportunity to familiarize myself with Azure DevOps, which proved to be a valuable platform for planning, coordinating development tasks, testing code changes, and ensuring the delivery of a high-quality software product.

To monitor the application's performance during stress tests, I utilized Datadog, a monitoring and analytics tool renowned for its ability to gauge performance metrics. This software enabled me to oversee various server parameters, including CPU usage, memory allocation, disk latency, and more, under different load test scenarios.


E-Manifest Project

During my time on TMMC, I took on the responsibility of researching, developing, and testing one of our Proof of Concept (POC) projects.

To give some background, TMMC currently uses paper manifests to document the build instructions for its vehicles on the assembly line and pick areas, which contribute to more than $170K of TMMC’s indirect costs in West Assembly.

To replace paper manifests and optimize cost efficiency, we tested low-cost technology solutions including an E-Paper display with Raspberry Pi, but encountered high image write times that exceed our acceptable threshold.

To solve this problem, I utilized the Toyota Business Practice (TBP) problem-solving tool which is used to find solutions to technical problems.

Employing a combination of Python and C programming on the Raspberry Pi, I was able to effectively deploy custom manifest image to different E-Paper models. After testing various models of the E-Paper display, I was able to identify the model with enhanced hardware and software capabilities that facilitated significantly faster image refresh times. This breakthrough led to a substantial reduction in image write times, aligning with TMMC’s production line requirements while optimizing cost.

Goals and Reflections

During my co-op as a Student IT Consultant at CCS, I identified 4 goals that I wanted to achieve by the end of my co-op term.

Enhance my oral communication skills

Effective oral communication is a fundamental skill at a workplace and our day-to-day life as it helps us deliver and understand information quickly and accurately. At this job, good oral communication skills were crucial as we assisted several clients via the phone and at the IT help desk. Going into this job I was already fairly confident with my written communication skills, and I realized that the best way to improve my verbal communication skills was practicing. I took the initiative to take on more calls and soon began to enjoy helping clients as I learned how to effectively communicate with a wide variety of users to resolve their issues.

I also made sure to ask questions to my supervisor and team members when needed, especially when I needed more clarity on which departments or teams I would need to direct clients to for certain inquires that were out of our scope or needed to be escalated.
Additionally, participating in our team meetings gave me the confidence to openly share my ideas, suggestions, and ask questions.

Getting well versed in software and tools used by CCS to increase productivity

I was introduced to several new software during my co-op term at CCS. Some of the ones I used on a daily basis include:

  • Footprints – our ticketing system that allowed us to create, manage, and respond to client inquires and issues. CCS also uses this sytem to host its Knowledge Base
  • Active Directory (AD) – directory service that stores information about network components and allows us to manage user accounts including usernames and passwords
  • Lightweight Directory Access Protocol (LDAP) – directory protocol that we mostly used for locating user accounts, verifying information, and resetting account passwords.
  • Click4Assistance – live chat platform that allowed clients to contact us and get a quick response/solution to their problem or question.
  • Multifactor Authentication (MFA) – An authentication method that requires users to verify their identity using another device to access resources such as Microsoft 365 applications, and VPN-protected services such as network (shared) drives.
    • MFA was introduced to provide additional security for users and their accounts. It was important that we understood how to set up different authentication methods and how to troubleshoot issues so we could assist clients effectively.
    • I helped with testing the authentication methods, particularly the Microsoft Authenticator app and hardware security key. I also assisted with making documentation of the setup processes and troubleshooting steps for common issues users may encounter.
      Testing and documenting were especially valuable as I found that it had increased productivity as I could assist clients more quickly.

Improve my troubleshooting skills to effectively resolve client issues

One of the skills required for this job is the ability to troubleshoot, learn and synthesize new information quickly. I was presented with new issues on a regular basis which put my troubleshooting skills to the test.

When I encountered a new issue, I made sure I first clarified and understood what the problem was; this was especially crucial on the phone, where I cannot see the user’s device. I then utilized our documentation and the internet to see if there were any similar issues and possible solutions documented that I could implement. If I could not locate a viable solution, I communicated with my supervisor, team members or other teams in CCS to gain more insight on a particular issue.

A further step I took that simplified the troubleshooting process was to make note of the steps my team and I needed to take to resolve a new issue. This proved to be very beneficial as the next time I encountered the same or similar issue, I could refer back to my notes to assist the client efficiently.

I am now able effectively troubleshoot a wide variety of issues I encounter and assist clients to the best of my ability! I can confidently say that I was able to achieve this goal and it is a skill that I will be able to utilize and benefit from in future work terms as well.

Becoming a better leader

This job gave me the opportunity for leadership development as I was able to assist other CCS team members working with me, particularly at the IT help desk. As CCS had adopted some new procedures due to the IT incident, it was critical to keep all team members up to date. I took the initiative to inform team members about important changes, assisted them with learning new procedures, and answered any questions they had to the best of my ability.

Although I was able to improve my leadership skills during my 4-month co-op, I will continue working towards this goal as I was given the opportunity to stay with the team and continue working on a part-time basis. As a mentor, I will now be able to assist in training the new co-ops which will allow me to further enhance my leadership skills.

Key Takeaways

Cybersecurity is everyone’s responsibility

Throughout my co-op semester, I recognized the importance of cybersecurity for both organizations and end users. For organizations, it is crucial that they have robust security measures in place to help prevent infiltration of their network and systems. However, if users do not take the steps to follow good cybersecurity practices, it becomes increasingly difficult for an IT department to handle security incidents as the actions of individual users can significantly impact the security of an entire organization. For instance, if a user falls for a phishing scam and inadvertently gives away their login credentials, that can allow an attacker to gain access to sensitive data.

Learn to adapt to new technology and situations

Having a growth mindset during my co-op term allowed me to learn new skills and adapt to challenging situations. I feel that this was especially important as I faced new issues on a regular basis. I took on each problem as an opportunity to learn and enhance my technical knowledge and skills. This made me more confident in handling a wide variety of issues and increased my productivity and ability to adapt to new situations.

These takeaways will definitely help me move forward in my career as I believe they are crucial to succeed in the tech industry. My co-op experience has allowed me to become more aware of cyber security and increase efficiency by learning new technology!
